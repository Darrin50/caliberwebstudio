## Cluster Angle

The anchor post (`/blog/detroit-med-spa-website-design`) covers what goes on the website. This cluster answers the follow-on question every med spa owner actually has: *once the website is built, how does anyone find it?*

Local SEO for med spas is different from general local SEO in three ways: (1) GBP category selection is critical and non-obvious, (2) healthcare-adjacent businesses face specific Google policy constraints on review generation and promotional language, and (3) the Detroit market has a concentrated cluster of competing med spas in specific neighborhoods (Midtown, Birmingham, Troy corridor) that creates clear geographic targeting opportunities.

This post covers the Google Business Profile optimization system, local citation strategy, review velocity, and the website signals (schema, NAP consistency, service pages) that together determine whether a Detroit med spa ranks in the Maps 3-Pack for high-intent searches like "med spa near me" and "botox detroit."

## 5 Research Directives

The writer MUST find and cite all 5 before writing a single word of body copy. If fewer than 5 real data points can be sourced, flag the post to Darrin before proceeding — do not pad or fabricate.

1. **Live Google 3-Pack audit — "med spa detroit":** Search "med spa detroit" and "med spa near me" from a Detroit-area IP (or use Google's local search preview tool). Document the top 3 results: business name, review count, star rating, GBP primary category, whether a booking button is present, and how many photos the listing has. These are the actual competitors this post is writing about.

2. **Google Business Profile category research:** Find the current GBP primary category for "Medical spa" — confirm the exact category name and whether "Medical spa" is a distinct primary category or if med spas must use a nearby category (e.g., "Medical clinic," "Beauty salon," "Day spa"). Look for GBP category databases (PlePer Tools, GMB Spy, or similar) or Google's own category documentation. Also find 2–3 secondary categories Detroit med spas commonly use.

3. **Detroit-specific med spa search volume:** Find any publicly accessible estimate for "med spa detroit" monthly search volume — Google Trends relative index, SEMrush free overview, Ahrefs free keyword checker, or Ubersuggest. Document the source and the number (or range). If no hard data is available, use Google Trends seasonal index and document that explicitly.

4. **Local citation sources specific to Detroit/Michigan med aesthetics:** Find at least 3 Detroit-relevant citation sources beyond Yelp and Google: health directories (Healthgrades, Zocdoc, RealSelf for aesthetic practices), Michigan-specific business directories (Michigan.gov business listings, Detroit Chamber), and any Michigan aesthetics association directories. Confirm each source has a free or claimable business listing.

5. **Named Detroit med spas with strong GBP presence:** Find 2–3 real Detroit-area med spas with 100+ Google reviews and 4.5+ star ratings. Document their review count, photo count, and any visible GBP features (Posts, Q&A activity, booking integration). These are the benchmark examples the post will reference as what "winning" looks like.
