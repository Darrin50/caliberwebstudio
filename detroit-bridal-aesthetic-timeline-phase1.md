<h2>Why Michigan Brides Need a 12-Month Aesthetic Timeline</h2>

<p>Michigan wedding season peaks May through August, with June as the single busiest month in the state. That timing matters because several of the most-requested bridal treatments — laser hair removal especially — require more lead time than most brides realize until it's too late to act.</p>

<p>The brides who end up disappointed are rarely the ones who chose the wrong treatments. They're the ones who called a metro Detroit med spa six weeks before the ceremony asking about laser, Botox, and body contouring simultaneously. A 6-session laser series cannot be compressed into four weeks. A filler result you're unsure about cannot be revised in 10 days. The window matters.</p>

<p>This timeline gives you every treatment category mapped to the window where it actually works — with the real session counts, recovery buffers, and Detroit pricing you need to plan a bridal aesthetic program that delivers on the day.</p>

<h2>12–6 Months Before: Laser Hair Removal Is a Long Game</h2>

<p>Laser hair removal requires 4–6 sessions spaced approximately six weeks apart. Run the math: six sessions at six weeks each is 36 weeks — just under nine months. For a June wedding, that means beginning no later than September or October of the prior year. For a September wedding, January is your cutoff.</p>

<p>There's a second constraint beyond session count: laser hair removal should be completed before summer UV exposure. Post-treatment skin is more photosensitive, and melanin-rich skin faces elevated hyperpigmentation risk when treated close to significant UV. Detroit's UV index climbs past 7 by late May. If you're doing laser for a summer wedding, your last session needs to be behind you before May 1.</p>

<p>Metro Detroit pricing at <a href="https://www.drbeauty.com/menu-of-services-detroit/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Dr. Beauty in Detroit</a> runs $225 for a Brazilian, $300 for half legs, $500 for full legs per session. At 4–6 sessions per area for a meaningful result, budget the full series — a single session does almost nothing. Adorn Medical Spa in Dearborn (579 five-star reviews) and MUSE Medical Spa in Troy are among metro Detroit's most-reviewed laser providers. Ask any provider which platform they use and confirm documented experience with your specific skin tone.</p>

<h2>6–3 Months Before: Injectables — Botox, Fillers, and the Right Sequence</h2>

<p>Six months out is the right window to begin your injectable conversation — not because scheduling is difficult, but because you want runway to see results, assess them honestly, and make adjustments before the wedding is close enough to cause stress.</p>

<p><strong>Botox:</strong> Botox takes 10–14 days to reach full effect. Book your first appointment at 4–6 months out, review the results once they're fully settled, and schedule a touch-up at 6–8 weeks before the ceremony if any refinement is needed. That gives you two data points — a baseline and an adjustment — before the event, with no last-minute variables.</p>

<p>Metro Detroit Botox pricing runs from $8/unit at volume providers like <a href="https://alluremedical.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Allure Medical</a> (four metro Detroit locations) up to $10–$20/unit at Michigan Advanced Aesthetics in Royal Oak, and higher at Birmingham and Grosse Pointe boutique clinics. The full Detroit pricing breakdown is in the <a href="/blog/detroit-botox-cost-2026">Detroit Botox cost guide.</a> What to expect after your appointment — bruising timelines, swelling, what to avoid — is covered in detail in the <a href="/blog/botox-recovery-timeline-detroit">Botox recovery timeline.</a></p>

<p><strong>Dermal fillers:</strong> Lip filler, cheek filler, and undereye treatments all need more runway than Botox. Fillers can swell for 2–4 weeks and the final settled result isn't visible for a month or more. First filler appointment: at minimum 4 months before the ceremony. Touch-up if needed: 6–8 weeks out. The first-time-ever appointment is not the one to schedule at 8 weeks out.</p>

<h2>3–4 Months Before: Body Contouring Needs Recovery Runway</h2>

<p>CoolSculpting and Morpheus8 are not last-minute decisions. CoolSculpting works by eliminating fat cells — but those cells are processed by the lymphatic system over 8–12 weeks. The final result isn't visible until that window closes. Morpheus8 (radiofrequency microneedling) produces surface redness and swelling that resolves in 1–3 days, but skin remodeling continues for up to three months post-treatment.</p>

<ul style="margin:16px 0;padding-left:1.5em;">
<li style="margin-bottom:12px;"><strong>CoolSculpting:</strong> Schedule your full series 4–5 months before the ceremony so results are visible at 8–12 weeks post-final-session. Michigan Advanced Aesthetics and MUSE Medical Spa in Troy both offer CoolSculpting programs.</li>
<li style="margin-bottom:0;"><strong>Morpheus8:</strong> Schedule your last session at minimum 4 weeks before the wedding for full surface recovery. For a 3-session series, start 3–4 months out. Michigan Advanced Aesthetics in Royal Oak prices Morpheus8 at $1,500–$2,000 per session.</li>
</ul>

<p>If body contouring isn't part of your plan, use the extra lead time to schedule additional facial treatments or confirm your laser series is on track.</p>

<h2>2 Weeks Before: Your Facial Appointment</h2>

<p>Two weeks before the wedding is the ideal window for a HydraFacial or another low-downtime facial treatment. A HydraFacial delivers immediate, visible results — pore cleaning, hydration infusion, and a healthy surface glow — with zero recovery time. It's one of the few treatments that actually looks better the morning after the appointment than the same evening.</p>

<p>Dr. Beauty in Detroit prices HydraFacials at $150 and up. Theia Aesthetics in Midtown Detroit offers pre-event facial packages. Book early — the two-weeks-before window is the most competed time slot at every metro Detroit med spa during peak June wedding season. A full comparison of Detroit HydraFacial providers, pricing, and what to expect during the appointment is covered in the upcoming <a href="/blog/hydrafacial-detroit">HydraFacial in Detroit guide.</a></p>

<p>What to avoid at this stage: any injectable you haven't had at least twice before, microneedling, medium or deep chemical peels, or any laser treatment. If there's any meaningful risk of bruising, swelling, or an unpredictable response, this is not the week to discover it. Two weeks out is maintenance and polish — not experimentation.</p>

<h2>The Week Of: What to Do and What to Skip</h2>

<p>The week of your wedding is maintenance only. The rule is simple: if you haven't done a specific treatment at least twice before and know your response to it, don't do it for the first time this week.</p>

<p><strong>Safe this week:</strong></p>
<ul style="margin:12px 0;padding-left:1.5em;">
<li style="margin-bottom:8px;">Gentle facial massage or lymphatic drainage — reduces puffiness, no downtime</li>
<li style="margin-bottom:8px;">Brow tint or lash lift — only if you've had it before and your skin responds predictably</li>
<li style="margin-bottom:0;">IV hydration — only if you use it regularly with a provider you know</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Skip this week:</strong></p>
<ul style="margin:12px 0;padding-left:1.5em;">
<li style="margin-bottom:8px;">Any injectable — bruising risk and result uncertainty are unacceptable this close to the ceremony</li>
<li style="margin-bottom:8px;">Microneedling, laser, or any treatment with a recovery requirement</li>
<li style="margin-bottom:8px;">New skincare products — no new actives, formulas, or untested ingredients on your skin</li>
<li style="margin-bottom:0;">Any treatment you haven't had before — the week of a wedding is not an experiment window</li>
</ul>

<p>The goal this week is arriving at the venue exactly as planned. That only happens if you stopped making changes 2–4 weeks ago.</p>
