Here's what makes this situation both painful and fixable: **Dr. Shah has an outstanding clinical reputation that no one can find.**

The first thing a patient sees when they search her name is a dead website and a 3.49-star Groupon page. The 4.9 rating that took years to earn is buried where no one looks.

- Ageless Spa Medica

- Dr. Bhanu J. Shah Med Spa
- Ageless Aesthetics Medical Spa
- Woman to Woman Care Center

Every platform uses a different name. Every listing has a different phone number. Google's algorithm interprets this as a trust problem and ranks competitors with clean, consistent data above her.

She stopped running Groupon deals. Good decision. But the listing is still live, still public, still showing 3.49 stars and reviews that say "dirty" and "appointment cancelled without callback." She is paying the Groupon penalty — discounted reputation — without the Groupon revenue to offset it.
