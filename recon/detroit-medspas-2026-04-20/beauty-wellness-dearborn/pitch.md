## Problem 1: Your Phone Number Appears 10 Times on Your Own Website

We pulled your HTML source. Your phone number `(248) 917-1065` is literally repeated **10 times** in the header code alone. This is a Shopify theme bug — the platform you're on isn't built for service businesses, and it's shouting your number at visitors like a desperate flyer instead of a premium med spa.

*A prospect who Googled "med spa Dearborn" sees 10 phone numbers before they read a single word about what you offer. That's not confidence — that's clutter.*

## Problem 2: Your Website Was Built for a Retail Store, Not a Medical Spa

You're running a **Shopify website** — the same platform people use to sell candles and T-shirts online. Your URLs literally say `/pages/` and `/blogs/` like a product catalog. Shopify bakes in shopping cart logic, checkout flows, and inventory systems that load on every single page visit — slowing your site down and confusing search engines about what kind of business you are.

Your competitor **Adorn Medical Spa**, two miles away on Michigan Ave, runs on WordPress — a platform built for service providers. They look like a clinic. You look like a storefront.

*We build on Next.js — the same technology behind some of the fastest medical and service websites in the country. Your site would load in under a second and rank like a medical practice, not a Shopify store.*

## Problem 6: Google Can't Read Your Business Information

Your website has **zero structured data** (called "schema markup"). This is the code that tells Google your name, address, phone, hours, star rating, and services. Without it:

- Your reviews (4.7 stars, 146 reviews) don't show up as rich snippets in search results
- Google doesn't know your business hours — it can't display them on the results page
- Your services don't appear in Google's knowledge panel

*Adorn Medical Spa, with proper schema, gets star ratings, hours, and address previewed right in Google. You show a plain blue link.*

Your homepage H1 says "Michigan's Premier Medical Spa" **twice** — a technical error that tells Google your page is confused about its own topic.
