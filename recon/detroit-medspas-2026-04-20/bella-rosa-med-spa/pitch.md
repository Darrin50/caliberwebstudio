## The Situation in One Paragraph

Bella Rosa Med Spa is running a WordPress template built by a generalist marketing agency in 2023 and left untouched. The About page — live, indexed, and sending patients there — is about a fictional motivational speaker named Mark. The blog's only post contains the WordPress setup instructions as its body text. Every before/after photo on the site is recycled from device manufacturer brochures. There are four Google reviews total. The website has never ranked for "med spa West Bloomfield MI" and there is no plausible path for it to do so in its current state. Meanwhile, VIO, ESkinScience, and Aloria are capturing the exact patients that should be calling Bella Rosa.

---

## The Audit — Every Problem, Named

### The About Page Is a Life Coach's Page
Go to bellarosamedspa.com/about right now. The H2 reads: *"I'm Mark – Author, Speaker, Coach & Life Strategist."* The body copy is about someone who founded "Keynote" to "inspire emerging leaders." This is WordPress demo content that was installed in 2023 and never replaced. Every potential patient who wants to know who is treating them finds this instead.

**The fix:** A real About page with staff names, credentials, photos, and the story of why Bella Rosa exists. Patients choose a med spa based on trust in the provider — this page actively destroys that trust.

---

### Every Before/After Photo Is Borrowed from a Brochure
The filenames tell the story:
- `marketing_materials_BA-ElitePlus-D-McDaniel-HairRem-Pre-Post3Tx-01.jpg` — the words "marketing materials" are in the filename
- `Copy-of-Copy-of-PicoSure-B_A-Tattoo-7B.jpeg` — copied twice, renamed, uploaded
- `PicoSure_RSaluja_Post1Tx_1_Focus-300x169.jpg` — this is from a PicoSure clinical study

These images belong in a device manufacturer's sales deck, not a patient-facing website. When a prospective patient visits to see what results look like at *your* practice, they're seeing Cynosure's stock photography. There is zero proof that Bella Rosa has treated anyone.

**The fix:** A real before/after gallery built on actual patient results. Even 6-8 real photos from consenting patients would be a transformational improvement over zero. Paired with release form language and proper HIPAA handling.

---

### Google Can't Find You — And Here's Why

Not one page on the site has a meta description. Not the homepage. Not any service page. Google is writing its own descriptions from random body copy and the results look like this in search: a truncated sentence that tells nobody anything.

Beyond meta descriptions:
- Every page title is missing "West Bloomfield" and "MI"
- There is no structured data (schema) telling Google this is a medical business
- The sitemap hasn't been submitted since 2024
- The blog's only post is WordPress placeholder text — *indexed on Google right now*
- There are no city-specific landing pages for surrounding markets (Birmingham, Bloomfield Hills, Farmington)

**The fix:** A site architecture built around how West Bloomfield patients actually search. Individual treatment pages optimized per service and keyword. LocalBusiness and MedicalBusiness schema on every page. Real blog content that earns long-tail traffic. This is how VIO and ESkinScience built their search presence.

---

### There's a Syracuse Phone Number on Your Website

`/skin-care-specialist/` — a live, indexed page — displays two phone numbers: (248) 977-7791 and (315) 422-8331. The 315 area code is Syracuse, New York. This is copy-paste content from another client's site build that was never cleaned up. Any patient calling that number reaches a stranger in another state.

### You Have 4 Google Reviews. Your Competitors Have 100+

| Competitor | Google Reviews |
|---|---|
| VIO Med Spa (West Bloomfield) | 100+ |
| ESkinScience | 40+ |
| Aloria Skincare | 35+ (Yelp) |
| Michigan Med Spa | 20+ |
| **Bella Rosa** | **4** |

One of those 4 reviews says the patient *"tried numerous times to call and make an appointment but no one ever answered."* That review has no response strategy — it sits there as the dominant social proof signal for the practice.

**The fix:** Review capture is a website problem as much as an operations problem. Every modern competitor embeds a review widget, sends post-appointment follow-up links, and prominently displays their star rating on the homepage. Bella Rosa's site has none of this.

---

### Patients Can't Book — They Can Only Submit a Form

The "Schedule" button in navigation goes to a contact form. There is no real-time availability, no booking calendar, no confirmation email. A patient who decides at 9pm on a Tuesday that they want laser hair removal has no way to book — they have to wait for a callback. VIO shows available slots in real-time. ESkinScience uses PatientNow. Even the smallest competitors in the market have Vagaro or Acuity embedded.

**The fix:** A proper booking integration — Acuity, Jane App, or Vagaro — embedded directly in the site. Lower friction = more conversions, especially for the 35–50 demographic that books primarily on mobile after hours.

---

### No Staff = No Trust

Not a single staff member's name appears on the website. Not on the About page (it's about Mark the life coach). Not on any service page. Not in the footer. The only staff name found anywhere online is "nurse Katie" — mentioned in a Google review, not by the practice.

In a market where competitors prominently feature their licensed RNs, NPs, and medical directors with photos and credentials, Bella Rosa is asking patients to trust an unnamed, faceless practice with their bodies and their money.

**The fix:** Introduce your team. A single headshot and three sentences per provider immediately differentiates you from the template mill. Credentials shown = trust earned.

---

### No Privacy Policy, No HIPAA Notice

A medical practice collecting patient names, phone numbers, emails, and health-related inquiry details through a web form, with no published Privacy Policy and no HIPAA notice. This is legal exposure.
