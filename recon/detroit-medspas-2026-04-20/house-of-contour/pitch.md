## The Ammunition — What We Found

Use these specific pain points in the conversation. Each one is verifiable live, right in front of them.

### 1. The Counters Showing 0 (Show This First)
Pull up the homepage. The stat block reads "0+ Years Experience" and "0+ Clients." This is caused by a JavaScript conflict between SiteGround's optimizer plugin and Elementor's counter module. The target values are configured (3 years, 500 clients — though it should be 750) but the animation never fires. Every visitor sees zeros.

**The pivot:** "On our build, those counters work. 750 clients, 10,000 lbs lost, 203 reviews, 5.0 stars — all animated on scroll. Here's what it looks like."

### 2. The Wrong Address (Show This Second)
Open the contact page. The address listed in the body is 19400 Livernois Ave, Detroit — your old location. It's also in the footer of every page. A potential client looking for directions gets sent to a building you no longer work out of. Beyond the practical problem, Google reads two different addresses on your website (Southfield in the body, Detroit in the footer on every page) and it suppresses your local search ranking because it can't confirm which location is real.

**The pivot:** "Every page on our build has your correct Southfield address, in the schema markup, in the footer, everywhere Google and customers will look."

### 3. The Awards That Nobody Sees
Ask them: "Where are your awards on your website?" Most business owners think their About page is prominent. Walk them to the homepage and ask them to find the Forbes award or the Presidential recognition. They won't. The awards exist in a paragraph of body text on the About page — no badges, no visual, nothing on the homepage.

**The pivot:** "On our build, the first thing a visitor sees under your headline is your proof bar: '#1 Med Spa Metro Detroit 2023 · Forbes Black Business Mogul · Presidential Lifetime Achievement.' You earned those. They should be working for you."

### 4. The Invisible Reviews
203 five-star reviews and 27,000 Instagram followers — and your website doesn't reference either. Not a widget, not a badge, not a link to Google. The homepage has six anonymous testimonials with initials only, which read as unverifiable to a skeptical visitor.

**The pivot:** "We surface your real social proof front and center. Your Google rating embedded with a link to verify it. Your Instagram following shown. Real named reviews, not initials."

### 5. The Services Page That Says Nothing
Go to the Services page. The body copy reads: "We offer a wide variety of products and services for both male and female." That's literally the entire content. No descriptions, no pricing, no specifics. Worse, every service card links to an unbranded third-party booking URL — the experience for a new customer is: click service, get sent to a page that looks nothing like your brand.

**The pivot:** "Every service gets its own section with a real description, a booking CTA that keeps them in your world, and an individual page that can rank for 'body contouring Southfield' in Google."
