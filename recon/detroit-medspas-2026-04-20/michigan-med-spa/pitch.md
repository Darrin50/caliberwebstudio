## What's Hurting Them Right Now

### Problem 1: Demo Content Is Live
Their About page introduces "Jon Snow" as an Anesthesiologist and "Tony Stark" as a Cardiologist. These are Avada theme demo characters. Their own staff — a board-certified MD, a business director, and a master injector with years of experience — have no bios, no photos, no credentials on the site. Any potential patient who clicks "Meet The Team" is seeing a complete fabrication.

**What we fix:** Real staff bios with professional headshots, credential callouts, and trust-building copy for each team member.

### Problem 2: "Avada Health" Is Still On the Page
The About page heading literally reads "Avada Health" — a competitor theme's demo brand name. It looks unprofessional at best and deceptive at worst.

**What we fix:** Purpose-built About page with Michigan Med Spa branding, real values, real mission, real team.

### Problem 3: Google Sees Junk — Not a Med Spa
- Zero meta descriptions across the entire site
- Page title "Home New" is what Google indexes
- No structured data (LocalBusiness, MedSpa schema)
- Blog dead since 2018

The result: when someone searches "med spa West Bloomfield MI," the competitors with clean, modern, properly structured sites appear first. Michigan Med Spa is likely buried on page 2+.

**What we fix:** Full SEO buildout — meta tags, schema markup, keyword-targeted service pages, active blog infrastructure.

### Problem 4: 2.7-Star Public Reputation
Dr. Bleiberg's public profile shows a 2.7/5 rating. The reviews cite long wait times and front-desk attitude — issues the website can't fix. But what the website CAN do is build the perception of excellence before the patient ever calls — and set up a review request workflow to amplify the good experiences.

**What we fix:** Trust-forward design, prominent real testimonials, a post-visit review funnel built into the contact/confirmation flow.

### Problem 5: No Mobile Conversion Path
There is no sticky "Call Now" or "Book Now" bar on mobile. In 2026, the majority of med spa searches happen on phones. A patient finds the site, scrolls around confused, hits the outdated 2018 design, and books with VIO Med Spa next door instead.

**What we fix:** Mobile-first design with sticky call bar, click-to-call prominence, and a booking flow.
