## The Core Problem

Your current GoDaddy site is costing you patients every single day. When someone in Milford searches "botox near me" or "lip filler Oakland County," your site doesn't appear — because GoDaddy's builder never gave you the SEO foundation needed to rank. On top of that, every before/after photo on your homepage is broken and shows as a blank box, which means your strongest sales tool — your results — is completely invisible to new visitors. You've built a real reputation (5 stars, 61 reviews) and a real practice, but your website isn't carrying any of that weight.

A 5-star reputation that lives only on Google and Instagram is fragile. A new site that embeds those reviews, tells Rachel's 13-year RN story with authority, shows real before/after results, and ranks for the treatments she performs turns that reputation into a 24/7 patient acquisition machine. The goal is to build a site that's as good as her reviews say she is.

Position Caliber Web Studio not as a web designer but as a reputation protection and patient acquisition investment. ## Suggested Outreach Channel

**Instagram DM — primary, with email follow-up.**

Rachel is actively managing `@modernaestheticsofmichigan` on Instagram — this is her primary marketing investment and where she clearly spends her professional energy. A DM lands in the context she's already working in, feels personal rather than cold, and doesn't require her to check a separate inbox. Keep it under 5 sentences. Do not use a template-sounding opener. Reference something specific you saw on her profile (a specific treatment post, a before/after she shared, a reel) to prove you're not a mass-spam account.

Follow-up: if no DM reply in 72 hours, send a short email to `ModernAestheticsOfMichigan@gmail.com` that references the DM and includes a single-sentence hook about the broken images. The broken image issue is visceral — business owners understand it immediately and feel it.

Do not cold call as a first touch. For a solo RN running an appointment-only practice, unexpected calls during a treatment session are friction, not opportunity.
